Why these five disciplines

Safety isn't one job. It's five overlapping ones, running at once.

On a live asset, a process upset, a heat-stress incident, and a site evacuation can all be unfolding in the same hour — and each calls on a different discipline, a different standard, and a different team. These applications were built to mirror that reality: structured around the five pillars that actually run a safety case, not a single generic "EHS" bucket.

Occupational Safety

The everyday discipline of keeping people safe at the point of work — permit-to-work integrity, working-at-height controls, confined space entry, and the behavioural observation programs that catch drift before it becomes an incident.

PTW · BBS · LIFE-SAVING RULES

Process Safety

The engineering discipline of preventing loss of containment — HAZOP and bowtie barrier analysis, layers of protection, safety-critical element verification, and the management-of-change rigor that keeps a design intent from quietly eroding over twenty years of plant life.

HAZOP · LOPA · SCE INTEGRITY

Industrial Hygiene

The science of exposure — noise, vapour, particulate, heat, and radiation — measured, modelled, and controlled before they become an occupational illness statistic rather than a recordable incident.

EXPOSURE MONITORING · IH SURVEYS

Business Continuity

The discipline of keeping the organisation running when an operation can't — business impact analysis, recovery strategies, and the continuity plans that get tested long before the day they're actually needed.

BIA · RECOVERY STRATEGY

Crisis & Emergency Response

The discipline that's only ever tested under pressure — incident command structure, tiered emergency response, mass notification, and the scenario drills that turn a written plan into muscle memory for the people who'll actually run it.

INCIDENT COMMAND · TIERED RESPONSE · SCENARIO DRILLS
